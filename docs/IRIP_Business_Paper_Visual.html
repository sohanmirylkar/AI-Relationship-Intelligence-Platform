AI Relationship Intelligence Platform

A guided internal operating platform for investor relations, CRM quality, research automation, relationship intelligence, client deliverables, and AI cost governance.

Product thesis One investor meeting should immediately become structured, reviewable, CRM-ready, searchable, measurable relationship intelligence.

1. Executive Summary

IRIP is a business operations platform built for relationship-driven asset-management teams. It converts unstructured investor activity into structured, reviewable, CRM-ready, source-backed, and measurable intelligence.

The product is designed around the daily operating reality of investor relations and private-credit business development: meetings produce notes, notes produce follow-ups, follow-ups require CRM discipline, CRM discipline requires data quality, and management needs proof that AI is saving time and money rather than only generating text.

2. Business Problem

Investor relations teams manage high-value but fragmented information. A single allocator or consultant meeting can produce notes, action items, follow-up materials, CRM updates, research needs, and relationship context. When these activities are handled manually, the organization faces productivity loss and data-quality risk.

8. AI And Cloud Strategy

AI Provider Strategy

IRIP uses a provider abstraction layer. Anthropic and OpenAI can both be used through environment variables. This protects the organization from hard dependency on one model provider and creates room for provider comparison.

Local deterministic fallback exists for testing, development, and offline demos.

Anthropic Messages API OpenAI Responses API Local fallback

Cloud Strategy

Supabase is used as the open-source cloud provider for CRM export. It is Postgres-backed, supports REST access, and can later support authentication, storage, row-level security, and audit trails.

9. Data Model And Governance

The product data model is designed to support business traceability. Meetings create interactions, interactions create action items, documents create chunks, and CRM exports preserve external IDs.

Figure 6: Simplified Business Data Model

Governance Controls

  • API keys are configured through environment variables or deployment secrets.
  • Raw transcripts are not intended to be logged in application logs.
  • CRM sync requires validation before export.
  • Research output is designed to use source citations.
  • LinkedIn scraping is intentionally excluded.
  • Production Supabase use should include row-level security and service-key rotation.

11. IRIP Copilot

IRIP Copilot is the guided assistant built into the Streamlit sidebar. It makes the platform easier to use by answering practical workflow questions at every step.

Figure 8: Copilot Context Model

Copilot can answer questions such as:

  • What have I done so far?
  • What should I do next?
  • Where should I do it in the app?
  • Why is this CRM record not ready?
  • What does this dashboard metric mean?
  • How should I explain this workflow to management?

15. Organization Demo Script

The product should be presented as an internal operating platform, not as a standalone chatbot.

Opening line: IRIP was built to solve a practical asset-management operations problem: one investor meeting should not create manual CRM upkeep, duplicate records, delayed follow-up, scattered research, and invisible AI spend.
  1. Start in Meeting Intelligence and upload a sample transcript.
  2. Show summary, attendees, action items, CRM payload, and confidence.
  3. Ask IRIP Copilot what to do next.
  4. Move to CRM Autopilot and show duplicate warnings or required-field status.
  5. Export CSV or demonstrate Supabase sync readiness.
  6. Move to Knowledge Base and Investor Research to generate a cited memo.
  7. Generate a follow-up email or pitch outline.
  8. Show the Relationship Graph.
  9. Use Token Optimizer to show cost governance.
  10. End with Dashboard KPIs and the evaluation harness metrics.

17. Conclusion

IRIP demonstrates how AI can be used as an accountable business operating capability. It keeps humans in the loop, validates records before CRM export, supports source-backed research, tracks cost, guides users through workflows, and gives management measurable evidence of value.

The recommended next step is to pilot IRIP with synthetic and approved internal data, expand the evaluation set, validate the Supabase sync process, and identify the highest-value production workflows for deployment.
